# SSF Performance Layer

## Overview

The Performance Layer captures how characters exist and behave—the nuanced physical and vocal elements that bring them to life. This layer bridges the Vision Layer (what characters mean, what truth they must convey) and the Creative Layer (how characters look and sound). It documents the embodied reality of characters: how they move, speak, occupy space, and interact.

This enables consistent character portrayal across shots, provides detailed performance context for actors and collaborators, and gives AI systems complete information for generating or evaluating character depictions.

# PHYSICAL PERFORMANCE

## Character Physicality

### Physical Character Profile
Baseline physical existence of a character.

**Attributes:**
- Physical foundation:
  - Posture (upright, slouched, rigid, relaxed, asymmetric)
  - Center of gravity (high, low, forward, back)
  - Physical tension level (tense, relaxed, variable)
  - Energy quality (kinetic, still, restless, contained)
- Movement quality:
  - Speed (quick, slow, deliberate, erratic)
  - Fluidity (smooth, jerky, graceful, awkward)
  - Economy (efficient, wasteful, precise, sloppy)
  - Weight (light, heavy, grounded, floating)
- Physical confidence:
  - Spatial presence (takes up space, minimizes self)
  - Physical comfort (at home in body, disconnected)
  - Coordination level (coordinated, clumsy)
- Physical habits:
  - Recurring gestures
  - Comfort behaviors (self-soothing, fidgeting)
  - Nervous tells
  - Signature movements
- Physical limitations:
  - Injuries, disabilities
  - Age-related physicality
  - Condition-related movement
- Physical history:
  - How body tells character's story
  - Physical training visible (athlete, dancer, soldier)
  - Physical neglect visible
  - Injuries/trauma visible in movement

**Links to:**
- Character (base entity)
- Character arc (how physicality changes)
- Vision Layer (what physicality expresses thematically)

# QUERY EXAMPLES

### Complete Character Performance Profile
"Get full performance profile for Flippy"

Returns:
- **Physical baseline:**
  - Posture: Slouched, shoulders forward (protective, closed off)
  - Movement: Quick, efficient, tries not to take up space
  - Tension: Holds in shoulders and jaw
  - Habits: Picks at fingernails, avoids eye contact, hair-touching when nervous
  - Physicality transforms: Early scenes vs. late scenes show opening up
  
- **Vocal baseline:**
  - Pitch: Mid-range, tends to go quieter when uncomfortable
  - Pace: Fast when nervous (wants to be done talking), slows when genuine
  - Patterns: Lots of "like" and "whatever" as shields, drops these when real
  - Articulation: Mumbles when hiding, clear when connecting
  
- **Key performance notes:**
  - Less is more—small, real reactions trump big choices
  - Stillness is powerful for this character
  - Physical comfort with skateboard is opposite of social discomfort
  - Voice should nearly break in Scene 23, but not fully

### Scene Performance Context
"Get performance guidance for Scene 23"

Returns:
- **Flippy physical:**
  - Opens tense, slightly turned away, picking at fingernails
  - Stillness when he starts talking (forcing himself to stay)
  - Small rocking, self-soothing
  - Gradual physical opening through scene
  - Ends: shoulders dropped, facing Sarah, still but relaxed
  
- **Flippy vocal:**
  - Quiet. Don't project.
  - Voice can be unsteady, might catch
  - Long pauses are okay—he's finding words
  - No "performing emotion"—let it be awkward and real
  
- **Sarah physical:**
  - Open posture, but not crowding him
  - Stillness that gives him space
  - Small forward leans as she engages
  - One careful touch at end (if it feels earned)
  
- **Blocking:**
  - Opens with table between them
  - He moves closer during speech (removing barriers)
  - Ends sitting on floor together—level, nothing between them
  
- **Proxemic arc:** Social distance → Personal distance → Intimate distance

### Physical Relationship Tracking
"How does physical relationship between Flippy and Sarah evolve?"

Returns:
- **Act 1:**
  - Distance: Social/public distance maintained
  - Touch: None
  - Blocking: Obstacles between them (tables, other people)
  - Mirroring: None (bodies speaking different languages)
  
- **Act 2:**
  - Distance: Gradually closing to personal distance
  - Touch: Accidental brushes, she initiates casual touch
  - Blocking: Start sharing space, walking together
  - Mirroring: Beginning to match rhythms, postures
  
- **Act 3:**
  - Distance: Comfortable at intimate distance
  - Touch: Natural, casual, both initiate
  - Blocking: Move as unit, physical ease together
  - Mirroring: Bodies in sync, completing each other's gestures
